<p><p>The Haemophilia treatment market is primarily categorized into two types: Haemophilia A and Haemophilia B. Haemophilia A, caused by a deficiency in clotting factor VIII, is the more prevalent form, and treatments often include factor replacement therapies and gene therapy. Haemophilia B, resulting from a deficiency in factor IX, has a smaller patient population but also utilizes factor replacement therapies and innovative treatment options like extended half-life products. Both segments are witnessing advancements, enhancing patient outcomes and quality of life.</p></p>
