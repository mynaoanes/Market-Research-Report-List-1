<p><p>Pellet fuel is a type of renewable energy made from compressed organic materials, primarily wood waste, sawdust, and agricultural residues. This biomass product is typically in the form of small pellets that can be easily transported, stored, and used in various heating systems, including residential stoves and large industrial boilers. The adoption of pellet fuel is accelerating as consumers and businesses seek sustainable energy alternatives to fossil fuels.</p><p>The Pellet Fuel Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.1% during the forecast period. Furthermore, the demand for cleaner heating solutions and the rising cost of conventional fuels are propelling the shift towards pellet fuel. </p><p>Key trends include the expansion of pellet production facilities, the integration of pellet fuel in combined heat and power (CHP) applications, and innovations in pellet manufacturing processes for efficiency. Global regions, particularly Europe and North America, are leading the way in embracing pellet fuel, while emerging markets are beginning to recognize its benefits for sustainable energy.
