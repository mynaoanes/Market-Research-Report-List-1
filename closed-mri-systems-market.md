<p><p>Closed MRI systems are categorized based on their magnetic field strength, affecting image quality and diagnostic capabilities. Low-to-mid field systems (0.2 to 1.5 Tesla) provide basic imaging suitable for routine examinations. High-field systems (1.5 to 3 Tesla) deliver enhanced resolution and faster scans, making them ideal for specialized diagnostics. Very high field (3 to 7 Tesla) systems further improve detail for advanced studies, while ultra-high field systems (above 7 Tesla) are used primarily in research settings, offering unprecedented clarity.</p></p>
